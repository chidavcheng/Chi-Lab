## Teaching Philosophy

I believe that great engineering education goes beyond conveying facts — it should inspire curiosity, build confidence, and equip students with the tools to tackle real-world challenges. My teaching is guided by three principles:

- **Active learning** — I design courses around hands-on experiments, problem-solving, and real data, so students learn by doing rather than passive listening.
- **Adaptability** — I continuously refine my courses based on student feedback, adjusting pace, style, and content to maximise engagement and understanding.
- **Accessibility** — I strive to make complex concepts approachable for all students, regardless of background, through clear explanations and a supportive classroom environment.

These principles are reflected in my completion of UNSW's [Course Design Institute (CDI)](https://www.unsw.edu.au/teaching/professional-development/course-design-institute) and the [Kaufman Teaching Certificate Program (KTCP)](https://tll.mit.edu/programming/grad-student-programming/kaufman-teaching-certificate-program/) at MIT.

## Courses at UNSW Sydney

### [CEIC2001 — Fluid & Particle Mechanics](https://www.handbook.unsw.edu.au/undergraduate/courses/2026/CEIC2001)
*Course Convenor & Instructor, School of Chemical Engineering, UNSW Sydney*

This course introduces students to the fundamentals of fluid mechanics and particle dynamics, with applications in chemical engineering. Topics include viscous flow, pipe systems, pumps, fluidisation, and sedimentation.

### [CEIC8341 — Membrane Processes](https://www.handbook.unsw.edu.au/postgraduate/courses/2026/CEIC8341?year=2026)
*Course Coordinator & Instructor, School of Chemical Engineering, UNSW Sydney*

This postgraduate course covers the fundamentals and applications of membrane technology, including membrane materials, transport mechanisms, and industrial separation processes. Topics include microfiltration, ultrafiltration, nanofiltration, reverse osmosis, gas separation, and membrane distillation.
