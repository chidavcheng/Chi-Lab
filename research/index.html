research

Research themes of the Chi Lab at UNSW Sydney.

Our Research

The Chi Lab works at the intersection of nanoscience and chemical engineering. We design atomically thin membranes, uncover the physics of molecular and ionic transport at the nanoscale, and translate these discoveries into solutions for chemical separations, green energy, and sustainable resources.

Nanofluidics & Ion Transport

We study how molecules and ions move through angstrom-scale pores and confined spaces, revealing sub-continuum transport physics that underpins next-generation separation technologies.

Atomically Thin Membranes

We engineer nanoporous graphene and related 2D materials as ultrathin membranes with precisely controlled pore sizes, enabling ultrafast and highly selective molecular separations.

Chemical Separations

We develop membrane-based solutions for organic solvent nanofiltration, gas separation, and ion-selective transport — targeting energy-efficient alternatives to conventional separation processes.

Green Energy

We apply nanofluidic membrane electrode assemblies for green hydrogen production and energy storage, contributing to a cleaner and more sustainable energy future.

Sustainable Resources

We address critical challenges in resource security — from lithium extraction and water purification to chemical recycling — using advanced membrane technologies to close material loops.

Transport Modelling

We combine experiment and modelling to understand and predict molecular and ionic transport in confined geometries, informing the rational design of next-generation membrane systems.

Funding & Affiliations

Our research is supported by the Australian Research Council, the Department of Education Trailblazer Program, UNSW Sydney, and international collaborators at MIT and the Chinese Academy of Sciences. We are affiliated with the UNESCO Centre for Membrane Science and Technology.
